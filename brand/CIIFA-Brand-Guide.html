CIIFA

Creative Industries Initiative for Africa — visual identity and document standards. One system for the website, decks, documents, spreadsheets and everything the world sees.

01The brand in one paragraph

CIIFA builds the systems that turn African creative talent into an industry. The brand should feel the way the work feels: institutional confidence with creative energy — dark, calm, serious surfaces lit by a single electric lime. Never corporate-beige, never carnival. One accent, used with discipline, is the whole trick. The official tagline: "Driving Africa's Creative Economy Forward."

The logo

The CIIFA lockup: "CIIF" wordmark with the lime Africa glyph completing the "A", over "CREATIVE INDUSTRIES INITIATIVE FOR AFRICA" in letterspaced caps. The Africa glyph's lime #90E040 is the master accent of this whole system. Use the black-text version on light surfaces and the light-text version on Ink; never recolour the glyph.

The one rule that matters

Lime is a light — not a reading colour. On white or Paper, lime text fails contrast; use Deep Green #46760F for links, emphasis and small graphics on light backgrounds, and save lime for dark surfaces, large shapes, bars and highlights. On Ink, lime can do anything.

Platform accents

Each platform owns one colour, used beside — never instead of — the CIIFA core. Status colours (red #c0392b · amber #b8860b · green #2e7d52) are reserved for RAG/status only.

Naming & attribution

First mention is always the full name: Creative Industries Initiative for Africa (CIIFA), then CIIFA. Programme attributions are fixed phrases: “CBA for Africa — executed by CIIFA for the Creative Talent Foundation”, “CIIFA presents The Skill Pool Initiative”, and co-delivered bootcamps read “Business of Music — delivered with [Partner]”.

05Do & don't

Do
  • Use lime sparingly — one accent per composition.
  • Use Deep Green for anything that must be read on white.
  • Let dark surfaces (Ink) carry the drama; keep light pages calm.
  • Pair each platform's accent with the CIIFA core, consistently.
  • Leave whitespace. Institutional means unhurried.
Don't
  • Don't set body text in lime, on any background.
  • Don't mix platform accents in one layout without reason.
  • Don't use amber/red/green status colours decoratively.
  • Don't stretch, recolour or outline the logo (once uploaded).
  • Don't introduce new typefaces or gradients per document.
