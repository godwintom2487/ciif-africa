The Enterprise Programme

Africa's creative industries are talent-rich and company-poor. That's not a talent failure — it's a business gap, and it's teachable. In four weekends.

4-weekend intensives 20–25 seats per cohort In-person + CIIFA LMS No exams — only deliverables AMEDI graduation showcase

What it is

The Enterprise Programme is a set of vertical business bootcamps for working creatives — artists and managers, filmmakers and producers, designers and brand founders. Every week ends in a real, ownable deliverable: signed split sheets, tech packs and costing sheets, release rollout plans, buyer pitches. Graduates leave with an investment-ready plan, not a certificate on a shelf.

The 2026 pilots — Lagos

  • Business of Music — 5–27 September 2026. Structuring the African sound for global export: identity & export strategy, rights management, street-to-stream release rollout, and revenue — including the December economy.
  • Business of Fashion — 3–25 October 2026. From sketch to storefront: collection planning, production & costing, branding, and sales strategy — leaving with tech packs, a lookbook and a buyer pitch.
  • Business of Film — dates to be announced. From film concept to investment-ready business plan: packaging, financing, distribution, and the art of the pitch.

Both pilot cohorts graduate at the AMEDI showcase in November 2026. 2026 is the education year — funded programmes and the Greenlight arrive in 2027.

Co-deliver it with us

Each vertical runs with one industry delivery partner who brings faculty, space and reach — on a transparent revenue share, with co-billing and first right on the 2027 funded edition. See how partnership works →
